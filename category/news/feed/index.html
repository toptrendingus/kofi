An office order has been issued in this regard from the ministry on Tuesday (January 16) night.

In the order regarding the temporary closure of academic activities of educational institutions during the current cold weather, it is said that cold weather is currently ongoing in various districts of the country. The normal activities of education of students are being disrupted in this winter. In the districts where the maximum temperature will drop below 10 degrees Celsius (according to the weather forecast of the concerned Meteorological Office), the Deputy Directors of the concerned Departments in consultation with the District Primary Education Officer of the concerned Districts will direct the academic activities of the Government Primary Schools of the concerned districts to be temporarily closed taking into account the severity of the winter and the local reality. (up to a maximum temperature of 10°C or above).

It further said that this order was issued in public interest, which will remain in force till January 31.

Earlier, the Directorate of Secondary and Higher Education issued an order stating that schools and colleges can be closed if the temperature is below 10 degrees Celsius.

FIFA not only honored Marta Vieira de Silva for her long career, but also gave great recognition. The name of the Brazilian great has announced a new award of women’s football – ‘Marta Award.’

Marta holds the record for the highest number of goals scored by men and women in the World Cup with 17 goals. He is also the first player to score five consecutive goals on the world stage of football. FIFA honored him with a special trophy at The Best FIFA Football Awards in London on Monday night for his contribution to football for more than 20 years. It was there that the award of the best goal of the year was announced under the name of the women’s football superstar.

Marta also once held the record for both men’s and women’s FIFA Footballer of the Year awards, winning six times. Later he was overtaken by Argentine superstar Lionel Messi. This is the eighth time he has won this year.

FIFA has an award for the best goal of the year. Since 2009, they have been giving the Puskas Award for spectacular goals. The first was won by Portuguese superstar Cristiano Ronaldo. For a stunning goal from 40 yards for Manchester United in the Champions League quarter-final against Porto.

Real Madrid and Hungary legend Puskas could not see the award launched in his name. He died in 2006, the award was launched in October 2009.

Both male and female footballers were considered for the award, but for the first two years only men were in the top ten. USA’s Heather O’Reilly made it to the list of top 10 goals for her great goal against Colombia in 2011 World Cup.

In 2014, a woman made the top three for the first time. Ireland’s Stephanie Rush finished second behind Colombia’s James Rodriguez. Since then, only two others – Daniuska Rodríguez (2016) and Denia Castianos (2017) from Venezuela – have made the top three.

The Puskas Award is determined by a vote of football fans registered on the FIFA website. There, male footballers who are far ahead in terms of fan base have an added advantage.

Alecia Russo was considered a strong contender last year to become the first female footballer to win the award. The English forward scored a stunning back-heel goal against Sweden at the Women’s Euros. But he could not come in the top three.

If the Puskas Award had been launched in the previous year instead of 2009, Marta might have been considered. He scored a brilliant goal in the 4-0 World Cup semi-final win over the USA. 

Marta is very happy to receive the award in her own name.

“Of course, it (becoming a record six FIFA Man of the Year) gives me great joy. But this recognition elevates not only Marta, but all of us—Brazil as a nation.”

“I will not play again in the World Cup. I am very happy with the way women’s football has developed in Brazil and in the world. There are incredibly talented girls who have great futures ahead of them. It’s just the beginning for them, but the end for me.”
